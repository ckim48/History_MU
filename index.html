Project Overview

Project MU aimed to develop a mobile application, to collect and analyze various datasets and to set marketing strategies based on the contents provided by our client, the Museum of Japanese Colonial History in Korea.

The museum application encompasses exhibition translations, videos on the history of historical colonial buildings, museum mascots, and more. Various dashboards and short essays on the colonial history were also created to aid visitors in understanding the colonial history of Korea.

Project Duration: July 24th, 2023 - Dec. 18th, 2023

Team Objectives

The primary objectives of the History & Data Analytics team were as follows:

  • Understanding historical colonial contents to translate and document materials provided by the museum for international visitors' comprehension of the museum's purpose and exhibitions.
  • Developing an analytical web-based dashboard to explore diverse perspectives on the Japanese colonial era, aiding visitors in deeper understanding.
  • Composing a concise paper on the Japanese colonial era to facilitate a comprehensive understanding of history.

Client Information

The Museum of Japanese Colonial History in Korea is the first museum to specialize in documenting and exhibiting the history of the anti-Japanese struggle during the Japanese colonial era. It aims to lead in overcoming the present-day Japanese colonial remnants and the divisional system of Korea to promote peace in East Asia and relies on the donations and donated data from citizens and overseas Koreans.

Translation

Initially, all museum information, including introductory and exhibition contents, was entirely in Korean, posing difficulty for foreign visitors to grasp the museum's purpose and exhibition content. The History & Data Analytics team translated introductory contents, feedback surveys, and permanent exhibition details. Additionally, the team recorded translated exhibition content for embedding into the museum mobile application as English audio guides.

About Data

This data has been obtained from the Japanese colonial museum in Korea. By collaborating with the museum, we were successful in initiating a survey that would ask visitors of the museum about their overview and thoughts about the museum.

The survey is categorized into four questions, where the survey would ask the visitors about their satisfaction scores, nationality, age range and personal opinions. Furthermore in order to express their personal opinions through graphs, personal opinions were evaluated by utilizing the sentiment scores.

Statistics

  • Average Visitors per day: 102
  • Number of countries: 12
  • Average Age of Visitors: 212
